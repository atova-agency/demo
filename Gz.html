Beyond choosing an app, your security depends on how you use these tools. Here are some key principles to follow:

  • Understand the Threat Model: No tool is 100% secure. The best choice depends on who you are trying to protect against and what you are trying to do. Using Tor to anonymously browse the web is very secure for the average user, but using a new app like Bitchat to organize a local protest carries different, less-understood risks.
  • Combine Tools for Strength: You can use tools together for layered security. For instance, you could use the Signal app over the Tor network to combine strong encryption with anonymity.
  • Prioritize Established Audits: Trust tools that have been around long enough for their security to be tested and verified by independent experts. The serious flaws in Bridgefy, which was once widely promoted for protests, were only exposed through such research.
  • Keep it Concise and Visual: Gen-Z naturally gravitates towards concise, visual communication. This is also a security advantage, as sharing less text-based information can reduce risk.
