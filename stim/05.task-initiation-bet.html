Immediate Action Protocol

Break paralysis in under 2 minutes

2-Minute Physical Movement
Purpose: Break mental paralysis through physical action
Options: Stand up stretch 30 seconds . Get glass of water . Take 3 deep breaths
Why: Changes physiological state, interrupts stuck pattern
Micro-Commitment
Purpose: Reduce barrier to starting
Instruction: Commit to just 5 minutes of work
Example: "I will just open document and look for 5 minutes"
Why: Small commitments feel manageable, often lead to continued work

Root Cause Analysis

Identify why you're stuck

Overwhelm
Symptom: Task feels too big . Don't know where to start
Solution: Break into microscopic steps
Perfectionism
Symptom: Fear of doing wrong or imperfectly
Solution: Aim for good-enough instead of perfect
Uncertainty
Symptom: Not enough information to begin
Solution: Identify first information gathering step
Lack of Energy
Symptom: Physically or mentally drained
Solution: Address energy before task

Concrete Starting Strategies

Targeted solutions for each blockage

Overwhelm - First Tiny Step
Purpose: Identify absolute smallest possible action
Why: Momentum creates more momentum
Examples:
• Write report → Open blank document type title
• Clean kitchen → Put away 3 items
• Exercise → Put on workout clothes
Perfectionism - Draft Zero
Purpose: Intentionally create bad first version
Why: Removes performance pressure
Instruction: Set goal to create worst possible version quickly
Example: "I will write worst report possible in 15 minutes"
Uncertainty - Information Hunt
Purpose: Make starting about gathering data not doing work
Why: Turns paralysis into productive research
Examples:
• List what I don't know
• Find one resource that might help
• Ask one clarifying question

Emergency Start Protocols

For when nothing else works

5-4-3-2-1 Countdown Method
Purpose: Use countdown to bypass overthinking
Steps:
1. Acknowledge you're stuck
2. Count backwards 5-4-3-2-1
3. On 1 physically move toward starting
4. Do not think - just act on 1
Body Doubling
Purpose: Work alongside someone else even virtually
Why: Social pressure creates accountability
Options:
• Call friend work on mute together
• Go to coffee shop or library
• Use focusmate or similar service

Using the Betting Timer

Celebrate exceeding your conservative goals

How the Betting Timer Works
Step 1: Set a conservative time bet (e.g., 5 minutes)
Step 2: Click "Set Bet" then "Start" to begin working
Step 3: Work until you naturally stop or complete the task
Step 4: Click "Stop" - if you worked longer than your bet, you win!
Psychology: Low pressure goals reduce resistance, exceeding them builds momentum
Betting Strategy Examples
Conservative Bets:
• "I bet I can work for just 5 minutes" → Actual: 15 minutes ✓ WIN
• "I bet I can write one paragraph" → Actual: Three paragraphs ✓ WIN
• "I bet I can research for 10 minutes" → Actual: 25 minutes ✓ WIN
Key: The bet is the minimum, the win is exceeding it
