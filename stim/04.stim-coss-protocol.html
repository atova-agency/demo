A comprehensive system for on-the-fly self-diagnosis and regulation using COSS structure.

Designed for light sensitivity: Dark theme, low contrast, reduced visual stimulation

Diagnostic Sequence

Step-by-step self-assessment

Step 1: Apply Kinetic Anchors
Use kinetic-COSS protocol to ground in physical reality
Step 2: Environmental Scan
Context: scan environment . Object: identify alert . Status: gather facts . Somatic: body scan . Kinetic: observe motion
Step 3: Label State
Identify current state: OVERWHELM | SEEKING | CRASH | ALERT | NEUTRAL
Step 4: Execute Stim Solution
Match to sensory need from catalog and execute appropriate stim
Step 5: Issue Self-Command
Use TKI signal protocol to define next physical action

Sensory Need Catalog

Visual Regulation Tools

Visual Retreat
Purpose: Reduce visual input
Primary Stim: Bat-in-the-Cave (eyes roll upward)
Discrete Option: Focus on small point . Close eyes 2 seconds
Use when: Visual overwhelm from lights, crowds, complex scenes
Visual Filtering
Purpose: Manage visual data flow
Primary Stim: Waterfall (finger flapping in visual field)
Discrete Option: Trace patterns on thigh . Fidget with ring
Use when: Too much visual information to process efficiently

Sensory Need Catalog

Auditory & Vestibular Regulation

Auditory Damping
Purpose: Reduce sound input
Primary Stim: Helicopter (hand motion near ears)
Discrete Option: Tragus pressure . Insert earplugs
Use when: Noise sensitivity, loud environments, auditory overwhelm
Vestibular Reset
Purpose: Release tension, system reboot
Primary Stim: Electro (head/neck shaking)
Discrete Option: Ankle rolls . Shoulder shrugs . Clench-release fists
Use when: Body tension, need for proprioceptive input, system reset

Crisis Protocol

Emergency override system

Activation Conditions:
State: SYSTEM CRASH OR State: OVERWHELM-RED
Immediate Action
Execute crisis stims without restriction or hesitation
Social Context
Ignore social discretion requirements completely
Priority
Prioritize system stabilization above all else
Permission granted: Full primary stim use authorized in crisis states

Maximum effectiveness tools

Frequency: As needed throughout day
